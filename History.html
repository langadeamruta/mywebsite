Club History

Any institution which has completed its 74 years successfully speaks for its flawless working capacity and its passion to work for the society. Rotary Club of Satara will soon be completing its 75 Glorious years! Rotary Club of Satara was charter on 24th July 1944. The Chief Minister of Mumbai (then called as Bombay) Province, Sir Dhanjisha Cooper was the Charter Club President and Shrimant Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj was the Charter Club Secretary. The organising club was Rotary Club of Bombay. 1994-95, the Golden Jubilee Year of Club was celebrated by conducting various social projects.


Rotary Star Bulletin :
From 1956 till 1960, ‘Rotary Star’, bulletin of Rotary Club of Satara was being published on monthly basis. The archives of bulletin have information about various projects conducted by Rotary Club of Satara over the period of time. As per one bulletin, club members helped in the construction of an 800 feet long, 10 feet wide and 1 foot thick road costing around ₹ 9,000 in Karanje area Satara. The archives have an entry stating, Shrimant Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj Museum came into existence due to Rotary Club of Satara’s initiative and a generous support from Late Shrimant Chhatrapati Rajamata Sumitraraje. The bulletin also has reference to the visits to Rotary clubs of Halifax, Kingston and Rome by Rtn. M. M. Doshi. Bulletin also has reference to help given to the club by D. S. Pawar, Rambhau Bichkar, S. S. Shete, M. K. Gite. According to the records, Respected Chief Minister of Mumbai (then called as Bombay) Province Mr. Yashvantrao Chavan laid the foundation of Rotary Garden, a project sponsored by Rotary.
Establishment of Rotary Movement :

Due to the tireless efforts of well-known people like P. V. Gole, Y. R. Phadke, K. M. Shaha, L. G. Kanhere, K. L. Doshi, A. K. Kulkarni, M. G. Bhagwat, R. K. Palkar, Rangrao Shalgar, Sam Cooper, Rajabhau Doshi and many more, Rotary Movement became a part of Satara City. It not only became a part but started growing rapidly.

Rich and Prosperous Heritage :

Rotary Volunteers :
Rotary Club of Satara is not just a group of well-known people, the club has extended its helping hand in various projects conducted by Rotary International as well. Past President (President 1992-93) Rtn. Dr. Prakash Chaukwale participated in the Rotary Volunteer Scheme. As a part of this initiative he worked, in Philippines for 6 months as dentist, without pay. For his remarkable work he received Rotary Volunteer Trophy from Rotary International. In year 2003-04 Rtn. Dr. Shriram Bhakare and Rtn. Dr. Aparna Bhakare served as Volunteers in South Africa where they performed around 400 successful eye surgeries.



Various Projects :
1. Prizes for Students : Every year Rotary Club of Satara, along with R. N. Godbole Trust, distributes prizes among bright students, who are in need of support. On these guidelines under Rotary Youth Services Program Mr. Nitin Kanase from Satara went abroad for studies with sponsorship from club.
2. Ganeshotsav : To maintain the true intention of Lokamanya Tilak behind starting Public Celebration of Ganeshotsav and to maintain discipline, dignity of the festival, Rotary Club of Satara started the Sundar Ganesh Spardha. This competition encourages Ganeshotsav Mandal to celebrate the festival in healthy, clean manner. With the help of Rtn. Bhavarlal Purohit each year club donates drinking water to all the devotees who come to see the Ganesh Visarjan procession.
3. Medical Services : During club’s 75 years from 1944 to 2019, Rotary Club of Satara has arranged numerous Eye Camps, Dental Camps, Vaccination Drives, Medical Camps for Police Department, Cancer Diagnosis Camps. The medical service camps arranged by club include Eye Camp conducted by Dr. M. C. Modi and Polio Corrective Surgery Camps as well. The most famous medical camp organised by club is Polio Vaccination Camp. For past 15-16 years Rotary International and World Health Organisation are conducting End Polio drive in India. An ayurvedic camp named ‘Sukhayu-Dirghayu’ is organised by club to spread the awareness about ayurvedic medicines.
4. Multipurpose Medical Service Facility : Rotary Club of Satara Charitable Trust has a purchased 10,000 sq. feet land to build multipurpose medical service facility.
5. Club organises Garba Dance Competitions, Legal Aid Seminars, Blood donation camps, free medical diagnosis camps.
6. Club gave free computer education to students from Pratapsinh High School, Satara. Most of the students in this school are from lower economic class. Club has also donated a computer to this school.
7. Club has donated Glow-Sign board to Civil Hospital, Satara. Club frequently donates food material, clothes to ‘Aashram Shala’ (Schools for kids from remote/tribal areas).
8. Club also donates school uniforms to students belonging to very lower economical class every year.
9. The list of projects, like Jaipur Foot, Writing, Singing, Drawing, Rangoli, Elocution Competitions, Tree Plantations, Food donation, Police Booth Donation, Milk Centre creations, Drinking Water Station creations, Taking care of educational expenses for girls from low income family, Planetarium on wheels, organising various lectures, is huge!
10. Club has donated Water Storage Tank in Sumitraraje Garden, Traffic Police Booth, and Water Storage Tank in Kanyashala.
11. The Rotary Garden in Tahsildar Office is developed by the club. Club has also developed another ‘Rotary Balodyan’ (Garden for kids) at Krantisinh Nana Patil Hospital (Civil Hospital) Satara. Former District Governor Rtn. Dr. Hariprasad Somani (Dist 3130 year 2006-07) donated ₹10,000 for this project.


New Rotary Club :
Rotary Club of Satara has always been active to spread the Rotary Movement in the society. Rotary Club of Satara is the sponsor club of Rotary Club of Karad, Rotary Club of Koregaon, and Rotary Club of Satara Camp. All these clubs are running successfully on their own now.

Honorary Members :
Club gives honorary memberships to prominent personalities in the society. Late Karmaveer Bhaurao Patil, Hon. Yashawantrao Chavan, Editor of newspaper ‘Dainik Ekya’ Suresh Palnitkar were given such honorary memberships. Shrimant Chhatrapati Abhaysinhraje Bhosale, son of chartered secretary Shrimant Chhatrapati Shahu Maharaj, was also the honorary member of the club in year 1995-96. For year 1996-97 the honorary membership was given to Mr. Subhashraoji Deshmukh who was the president of Satara Zilla Parishad.

Golden Moment :
The golden page in the history of Rotary Club of Satara marks the selection of Rtn. Adv. Ismail Patel as District Governor for year 2003-2004 for Rotary District 3130. Under his leadership club members participated in various programs like District Assembly, Installation of all the clubs in Satara Cops held together.

District Officers :
Rotary District 3130 has 12 revenue districts. Many past presidents have worked and are still working as District Officers in rotary district 3130.

Public Relation Grant :
Another important event in the history of Rotary Club of Satara is that it received a Public Relation Grant of ₹ 61,500/- from Rotary International. In year 2006-07 only 17 clubs from Rotary District 3130 received this grant. This grant helped the club in spreading the information about Rotary’s World-wide work through advertisements in newspapers, banners at important spots in city, local television channels etc.
International Youth Exchange (I. Y. E.) Program :

The proud moment for club was selection of Miss. Namita Pradip Shaha in I. Y. E. Program for 1 year stay in Germany in year 2007-08. At the same time Miss. Peya Propper from Germany was selected for 1 year stay in Rotary Family from our club. In year 2007-08, 21 students from I. Y. E. Program stayed in Rotary Families for 3 days. Various family trips to Koyananagar, Patan, Ozarde, Chalkewadi, Thoseghar, Sajjangad, Kas, Bamnoli, and Tapola were arranged during these days.

New District :
Year 2008-09 was very important in Club’s history. This year Rotary International District 3130 was divided in Rotary International Districts 3131 and 3132. Our club was associated with Rotary International District 3132. In year 2008-09 a team of 5 club members went to Brazil for group study. They received a very grand and warm welcome. In year 2010-11 Rotary Club of Satara along with Reserve Bank of India and State Bank of India Satara Branch held Coin Distribution Camp for citizens. Around 750 people participated in the camp. Total coins worth Rs. 12.5 Lacs were exchanged in the camp. Rt. Sunil Sapkal was the chairman of this project. In years 2011-12, 2012-13 medical check-up camps were held for family members of club members. In year 2012-13 water storage tanks were donated to various schools. Food packets were also donated to volunteers on Polio Vaccination Booths. In year 2013-14 Eye and Dental Check-up camp was held for students of Satara English Medium. Chalate Chalate (Walkathon for Senior Citizens) was also arranged in 2013-14. Polio Rally was also arranged in that year. A program, called as ‘Salute to Kalakar’, dedicated to Artists from Satara was also arranged in same year. In year 2014-15 began the project ‘Naivedyam’ under the leadership of Former District Governor Rtn. Adv. Ismail Patel. The project started on 1st February 2015. At that time the lunch was given to all the patients and their relatives in Aryangla Hospital at 12 p.m. on each Monday, Wednesday and Friday. In year 2016-17 from 1st march 2017 Naivedyam Praklpa was started on daily basis i.e. for 7 days a week. In year 2017-18 along with other various projects, Rotary Club of Satara completed Library Project at Shripatrao Patil High School and Jr. College. Club renovated the school’s library along with donating books worth ₹ 80,000.

These are some glimpses of all the work that Rotary Club of Satara is doing. Just like its glorious and successful past club definitely has a bright future too!
